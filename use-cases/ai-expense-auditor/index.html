The Problem

What Is an AI Expense Auditor?

Traditional expense report audit is a sampling game. Finance teams at mid-market and enterprise companies process hundreds or thousands of expense reports every month — flights, hotels, meals, vendor invoices, corporate card charges, mileage claims. But with manual review, they only audit 5-10% of reports. The math is brutal: if a company processes 2,000 expense reports per month and each takes 15 minutes to audit properly, that's 500 hours of auditor time — requiring 3 full-time employees just for travel and expense audit. So they sample. And the 90-95% of unaudited reports? That's where risky spend, out-of-policy spending, and duplicate reimbursements hide.

Expense management software like SAP Concur and Expensify automated expense submission and approval routing, but they didn't automate the audit itself. These rules-based expense management tools catch obvious duplicates and flag amounts above a static threshold, but they cannot read a receipt in full detail, understand your T&E policy in natural language, or detect a split purchase designed to circumvent a $500 approval limit. They are rules-based systems — not AI expense auditing. According to the Association of Certified Fraud Examiners (ACFE) 2024 Report, expense reimbursement fraud accounts for 21% of all occupational fraud schemes — with a median loss of $40,000 per incident. Expense audit automation closes this gap by reviewing every expense report, not just the 5-10% a human auditor can reach.

An AI expense auditor is different. It is an autonomous AI system built for expense audit automation — connecting to your ERP (NetSuite, SAP, Oracle, QuickBooks) and expense management platform (Concur, Expensify) to automate expense report audits at 100% coverage. It reads every submitted receipt using OCR receipt processing — scanned PDFs, mobile photos, email forwards, handwritten amounts in 50+ languages. It understands your T&E policy in natural language — spending limits by employee level, permissible expense categories, per-diem rates by city, receipt documentation requirements, and approval hierarchies — and enforces expense policy compliance on every line item. It runs anomaly detection across multiple patterns: duplicate receipt detection and duplicate invoice detection across departments, split purchase detection to catch circumvention of approval thresholds, out-of-policy spending identification, personal expenses miscategorized as business costs, inflated tips and service charges, and mileage verification against map data. Every expense receives a risk score (0-100), and every decision is documented in a complete audit trail. Finance teams go from auditing ~5% of reports to 100% coverage — reducing monthly audit cycle time from 160 hours to 8 hours, catching 3x more policy violations, and enabling finance audit automation that scales without adding headcount.

How Key Metrics Are Measured

  • Audit Coverage (5% → 100%) — Percentage of submitted expense reports that receive a full audit (receipt verification, policy compliance check, anomaly detection). Manual audit coverage from Deloitte T&E Survey 2025. COCO coverage measured as the percentage of reports that pass through all six anomaly detection patterns and the policy compliance engine.
  • Audit Cycle Time (160h → 8h) — Total person-hours spent on expense audit per month. Pre-COCO baseline based on: (number of expense reports per month) × (average manual audit time of 15 min) × (sampling rate of 10%). Post-COCO: AI processes 100% of reports automatically (~8 seconds each); human time is spent only on reviewing flagged items (approximately 8 hours/month for a 2,000-report volume).
  • Violation Detection Rate (3× improvement) — Comparison of policy violations detected per 1,000 expense reports under manual sampling vs. COCO AI. Pre-COCO: violations detected only within the 5-10% sample. Post-COCO: violations detected across 100% of reports using six anomaly patterns and natural-language policy understanding. The 3× factor is conservative — actual improvement ranges from 2.5× to 5× depending on industry, policy complexity, and previous sampling rigor.
  • Missed Anomaly Rate (-95%) — Reduction in anomalies that go undetected. Pre-COCO: 90-95% of reports are not audited, and even audited reports have a ~25% miss rate for non-obvious violations. Post-COCO: 100% of reports audited with consistent detection patterns. The 95% reduction represents the shift from ~92% of violations missed (sampling 10% with 75% accuracy) to ~5% missed (comprehensive AI audit with edge cases requiring human review).

Limitations & Caveats

  • Results vary by industry, policy complexity, and historical data quality. Organizations with well-documented T&E policies and clean ERP data see the fastest time-to-value. Highly regulated industries with complex multi-jurisdictional policies may require additional calibration time.
  • The 100% audit coverage applies to expense categories within the AI's trained scope: T&E, corporate card, vendor invoices, and project expenses. Novel expense types or highly specialized industry-specific expenses may require additional training data.
  • OCR accuracy varies by receipt quality. Printed receipts achieve 98%+ accuracy; handwritten receipts achieve 85-92% accuracy depending on legibility. Receipts in uncommon languages or with significant damage may require manual verification.
  • The AI requires approximately 1 week of initial training on your T&E policy, historical expense data, and corporate card feeds. During this period, it operates in flagging mode — identifying potential violations for human review without autonomous decisions.
  • As with any AI system, edge cases exist. COCO maintains a configurable risk threshold — expenses above this threshold are flagged for mandatory human review. We recommend starting with a conservative threshold (risk score ≥ 60) and adjusting based on observed accuracy.

Can AI detect duplicate receipts and split purchases?
Yes. Duplicate receipt detection and split purchase detection are two core capabilities of COCO's AI expense auditor — and two areas where rules-based expense management software consistently falls short. For duplicate detection, the AI performs fuzzy matching across all submitted expenses — same vendor with similar amounts, dates, or descriptions, even if submitted through different departments or in different time periods. For split purchase detection, the AI analyzes spending patterns to identify cases where the same vendor appears across multiple submissions within a configurable time window, with amounts just under approval thresholds. For example: a $480 vendor payment under "Marketing" and a $470 payment to the same vendor under "Operations" three days later — both just under a $500 approval limit. A rules engine sees two separate, compliant transactions. COCO's AI sees a likely split purchase and flags both for review. See all COCO use cases.
How does receipt matching automation work?
Receipt matching automation uses OCR (optical character recognition) to extract vendor name, date, amount, individual line items, tax, tip, and currency from receipt images — scanned PDFs, mobile photos, email forwards, or paper receipts. The AI then automatically matches each receipt to the corresponding credit card transaction or expense line item in your ERP or expense management system. COCO's OCR engine handles 50+ languages, handwritten amounts, and multi-currency receipts. What took a human auditor 3-5 minutes per receipt takes the AI under 10 seconds, with 98%+ accuracy on printed receipts. Receipt matching automation eliminates the most time-consuming step of manual expense audit — the physical verification of every receipt against every transaction — and is a foundational component of any effective expense report audit tool.
