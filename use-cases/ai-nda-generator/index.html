The Problem

What Is an AI NDA Generator?

Traditional NDA drafting consumes 20 hours per week for the average in-house legal team — drafting, customizing, and reviewing the most common contract type in business. Each NDA takes 45-90 minutes: find the right template, customize party names and scope, check governing law, align non-compete and non-solicit clauses, add carve-outs, cross-reference definitions, format, and send. For a team handling 15-20 NDAs per week, nearly half of a full-time attorney's capacity is spent on documents that are 90% boilerplate.

An AI NDA generator changes this by automating initial NDA drafting while keeping attorneys in control of every final agreement. Template libraries (Practical Law, LexisNexis) and DIY platforms only provide static NDA templates — you still manually customize every clause and verify jurisdiction alignment. A New York NDA template used for a California deal creates unenforceable non-compete provisions. A mutual NDA doesn't properly convert to a unilateral one without structural rework. Industry surveys consistently show contract drafting and review as a top productivity concern for in-house counsel.

COCO's AI NDA generator is a contract drafting automation platform that creates NDA drafts in 60 seconds. You provide deal context in natural language — "mutual NDA between Acme Corp and Beta LLC, covering Q4 JV discussions, 2-year term, Delaware law, standard carve-outs plus non-solicitation" — and the AI generates a complete, jurisdiction-specific NDA draft with customized party definitions, deal-specific scope, aligned cross-references, and jurisdiction-appropriate restrictive covenants.

Legal teams review AI-generated drafts in 5-10 minutes instead of drafting for 45-90 minutes — a ~90% reduction — while first-pass accuracy reaches 93% based on aggregated internal deployment data. Whether you need a mutual NDA, unilateral NDA, one-way NDA, or multi-party NDA, the AI handles clause architecture for attorney review and finalization, using your NDA templates, clause library, and legal playbook across 40+ jurisdictions.

Jurisdiction-Specific NDA & Governing Law Alignment

The AI understands enforceability rules for 40+ jurisdictions: all US states (with California, Delaware, New York, Texas-specific rule sets), UK, EU member states, Canada, Australia, Singapore, Hong Kong, Japan, UAE, and key offshore jurisdictions. Automatically aligns non-compete, non-solicit, choice of law, and forum selection clauses to local standards. A California-governed NDA uses fundamentally different restrictive covenant language than a New York one — the AI handles this jurisdiction alignment automatically, reducing the risk of unenforceable provisions from governing law mismatches.

📝

Clause Alignment & Enforceability Review Support

Every generated NDA draft is internally consistent. When the AI customizes the scope definition in Section 1, it automatically updates every cross-reference throughout the document. When the governing law changes from Delaware to UK, it adjusts the restrictive covenants, carve-outs, and dispute resolution clauses accordingly. Before finalizing each draft, the AI runs review flags for attorney consideration: Are restrictive covenants reasonable in scope and duration? Are carve-outs complete and jurisdiction-appropriate? Does the choice of law clause have a reasonable relationship to the parties? These flags help attorneys catch the "Section 3 refers to a definition that was removed in revision 2" errors that plague manually customized NDA templates — but final legal judgment always rests with the reviewing attorney.

Mutual NDA, Unilateral NDA & Multi-Party NDA Generation

The AI handles all NDA configurations: mutual NDA (bilateral, protecting both parties' confidential information), unilateral NDA (one-way NDA, protecting only the disclosing party), and multi-party NDA (three or more parties with independent confidentiality obligations). It automatically adjusts clause architecture — in a multi-party NDA, each party's obligations, carve-outs, and liability provisions are independently defined and cross-referenced. Converts between types: take a mutual NDA and generate a one-way NDA with proper structural changes — no manual rework required.

Legal Playbook Integration & Attorney Review Workflow

The AI follows your legal team's drafting playbook: when to use mutual vs. unilateral NDA, standard vs. aggressive carve-outs, preferred governing law and venue, and fallback positions for negotiation. Upload your legal playbook as a document and the AI applies it to every NDA generated. Built-in attorney review workflow ensures every AI-generated NDA is reviewed by a qualified lawyer before execution — the AI drafts, attorneys judge. When your playbook changes (new risk tolerance, updated jurisdiction preferences), upload the revised version — the AI applies new rules immediately to all future drafts.

  • NDA Generation Time (60 seconds) — Measured from natural language instruction submission to complete, review-ready NDA delivered. Measured across standard deal types and jurisdictions. "Substantive change" defined as: clause modification beyond formatting, definition scope adjustment, or enforceability fix. Individual results vary by NDA volume and complexity.

Limitations & Caveats

  • First-pass accuracy varies by jurisdiction and deal complexity. Standard bilateral NDAs in major jurisdictions (US, UK, EU) achieve 95%+ first-pass accuracy. Multi-party NDAs, cross-border deals with conflicting legal regimes, and highly novel deal structures may achieve 85-90% and require more thorough attorney review.
  • Template migration quality depends on the clarity and organization of uploaded materials. Well-organized clause libraries with clear preferred/fallback annotations produce the best results. Highly inconsistent historical templates or conflicting playbook instructions may require additional calibration time.
  • AI-generated NDAs should always be reviewed by a qualified attorney before execution — particularly for high-stakes deals, novel counterparty relationships, or agreements involving intellectual property rights beyond standard confidentiality. The AI drafts; attorneys judge. The 5-10 minute review cycle is appropriate for routine commercial NDAs; complex or high-value deals warrant more thorough review.
