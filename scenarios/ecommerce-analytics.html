Platform Silos

Three ways fragmented data costs you margin

Each platform tells you its version of the truth. None of them show you the combined picture that determines profitability.

📊

Every platform defines revenue differently

Shopify reports gross merchandise value. Amazon deducts fees before reporting. Walmart has its own structure. Four platforms, four revenue definitions, no way to compare margin per unit without manual normalization in a spreadsheet that takes hours to update.

📡

Demand signals are scattered everywhere

On-site search queries (Shopify), search term reports (Amazon), social engagement (Meta/TikTok), Google Trends — each channel signals demand differently. No single dashboard connects these dots. You're making inventory bets on partial information.

📦

Inventory allocation is a guessing game

You have 500 units. 300 to Amazon FBA? 150 to Shopify? 50 to Walmart? Today's answer is based on last month's sales — a lagging indicator. You're not factoring in current velocity, upcoming promotions, or competitor stockouts on each platform.

Core Workflows

Six analyses that reveal where your margin lives

01

Cross-Platform P&L

Gross revenue, marketplace fees, shipping, ad costs, net margin — by Shopify, Amazon, and Walmart. Which SKUs have the highest revenue but lowest net margin on each platform? The question every operator needs answered.

02

Demand Signal Intelligence

Combine on-site search (Shopify), Amazon search terms, social mentions, and Google Trends. Which products have rising demand across multiple signals but low inventory coverage? Internal + external signals in one view.

03

Inventory Allocation Optimization

1,000 units arriving. How to split across Amazon FBA, Shopify, and Walmart? InfiniSynapse factors sell-through velocity, margin per unit per channel, and competitor stockout status for profit-maximizing allocation.

04

Conversion Funnel Analysis

Compare impression → click → add-to-cart → purchase across Shopify and Amazon for your top 20 products. Where's the biggest drop-off on each platform? Which products convert better where?

05

Competitor Price & Assortment

Browser Use checks prices and stock status of your top competing ASINs daily. Flag price drops >10% or stockouts. Estimate revenue opportunity from out-of-stock competitors. Automated, not manual.

06

True ROAS by Platform

For each dollar of ad spend on Amazon Ads, Google Shopping, and Meta — how much net margin (not just revenue) did you generate? Break down by product category. Flag products where ad spend exceeds margin.

FAQ

Can InfiniSynapse connect to Shopify and Amazon?
Yes — database connection, CSV export, or API. InfiniSynapse joins Shopify sales, Amazon marketplace performance, ad platform spend, and social media metrics in a single query — giving you a cross-platform view no single marketplace dashboard provides.
How is this different from Shopify/Amazon analytics?
Shopify Analytics shows your Shopify store. Amazon Brand Analytics shows Amazon. Neither shows the combined picture: total sales across platforms, which platform drives the highest net margin after fees, or which products sell better where. InfiniSynapse unifies everything.
How does demand signal intelligence work?
InfiniSynapse combines internal signals (sales velocity, on-site search, cart abandonment, wishlists) with external signals via web search and Browser Use (competitor pricing, Google Trends, social sentiment). This multi-signal approach produces more accurate forecasts than historical sales alone.
What e-commerce metrics can I analyze?
Cross-platform P&L (revenue, fees, ad costs, net margin by platform/SKU), marketplace comparison, demand forecasting, conversion funnel analysis, inventory allocation optimization, and true ROAS (net margin / ad spend by platform and product).
Is my e-commerce data secure?
Yes. Air-Gapped private deployment keeps all order data, customer information, sales figures, and competitive analysis on your own servers. Essential for businesses handling customer PII, payment data, or operating under PCI DSS and GDPR.
