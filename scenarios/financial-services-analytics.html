Risk Assessment

Three critical problems with financial data analytics

Financial institutions don't lack data — they lack secure, connected, and accessible analytics. These problems are unique to regulated financial services.

Critical

Financial data is locked in silos — by regulatory design

Core banking, risk management, compliance, and customer systems are intentionally separated for security reasons. But this makes cross-system analysis — risk aggregation, fraud detection, regulatory reporting — painfully manual. A risk analyst asking "what's our aggregate CRE exposure across all lending books?" must pull data from 3+ loan systems and manually normalize exposure definitions.

High

Most AI analytics tools are SaaS-only — financial institutions can't use them

Julius AI, Hex, Powerdrill, and most modern AI analytics tools require uploading data to external cloud servers. For regulated financial institutions subject to GDPR, SOX, PCI DSS, and national banking regulations, this is a non-starter. The result: most AI analytics innovation is unavailable to the teams that need it most.

High

Fraud detection data is scattered across incompatible systems

Effective fraud detection requires joining transaction data, customer profiles, historical behavior, and external watchlists — all in different systems with different access controls. Most fraud analysts manually review transactions in one system, look up customers in another, and check watchlists in a third, limiting coverage and missing cross-source patterns.

Core Workflows

Five core financial services analytics workflows

The highest-frequency workflows risk, compliance, fraud, and investment teams run on InfiniSynapse.

Workflow 01 · High Frequency

Fraud Pattern Detection & Investigation

Ask: Identify transactions matching suspicious patterns — unusually large amounts, first-time beneficiary in high-risk jurisdiction, multiple transactions just below reporting thresholds. Cross-reference with internal watchlist and recent SAR filings. InfiniSynapse joins transaction data, customer profiles, historical behavior, and watchlist data — producing a prioritized investigation queue that normally takes days to compile manually.

Workflow 02

Risk Exposure Aggregation

Aggregate credit exposure by sector, geography, and product type. Compare against internal limits and regulatory concentration limits. Flag sectors exceeding 80% of limit. A quarterly 2-week process becomes a daily 2-minute check.

Workflow 03

Automated Compliance Reporting

Generate monthly dashboards: capital adequacy ratios, LCR, large exposure reporting, NPL ratio by segment. Pulls from core banking, risk, treasury, and compliance — all on local infrastructure with full audit trails.

Workflow 04

Portfolio & Investment Analytics

Analyze performance by asset class: total return, risk-adjusted return, sector allocation vs. benchmark, top/bottom contributors. Queries portfolio systems and market data feeds simultaneously.

Workflow 05

Customer Financial Behavior

Segment by transaction frequency, balances, product holdings, and life stage. Identify cross-sell opportunities, churn risk indicators, and product propensity — through plain-English queries rather than ML model development.

When InfiniSynapse is not the right choice for financial services

  • You're building production financial ML systems. If you need to build, train, and deploy fraud detection ML models, credit scoring models, or algorithmic trading systems, you need a data science platform like Databricks. InfiniSynapse is the analytics layer for business and risk teams — not a model development platform.
  • You already have a mature financial data platform with self-service BI. If your institution has invested in a modern data platform with governed financial data models and a BI layer that business users can access, InfiniSynapse may be additive rather than transformative.
  • Your analytics needs are single-system and well-defined. If every question lives in your core banking system's reporting module and regulatory reports are already automated, you're well-served. InfiniSynapse's value grows when questions span multiple financial systems that don't naturally connect.

InfiniSynapse creates the most value when cross-system analysis is currently manual, when data residency requirements prevent using SaaS AI tools, and when business and risk teams need self-service access without SQL or data engineering.
