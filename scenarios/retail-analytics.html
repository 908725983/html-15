Channel Silos

Three ways disconnected channels hide the full picture

Each channel knows its piece. No system sees the omnichannel customer, the cross-channel inventory, or the competitive pricing landscape.

🛒

You can't see the omnichannel customer

A customer browses online, buys in-store, joins loyalty, and later orders delivery. Your e-commerce platform sees the browse and delivery. POS sees the in-store purchase. Loyalty tracks points. None see the complete customer. Databricks identifies this need — but implementation requires months of pipeline work.

📦

Inventory decisions are channel-by-channel

E-commerce sees online stock. Store manager sees in-store inventory. Supply chain sees warehouse levels. When a product sells fast online but sits on store shelves, no system flags the imbalance — because no system sees both channels. Each channel optimizes in isolation.

💰

Pricing is reactive and channel-specific

Competitor drops price online — e-commerce team reacts days later. Product is overstocked in stores — markdowns happen at end-of-season, not when inventory signals first appear. Real-time pricing requires joining competitor data, inventory, demand, and margin across channels — data that's never in one place.

Core Workflows

Six analyses that connect every retail channel

01

Omnichannel Customer 360

Build unified profiles: in-store frequency, online history, loyalty tier, favorite categories per channel, cross-channel shopping patterns. POS + e-commerce + loyalty joined on customer identity — in days, not months.

02

Store-SKU Demand Forecasting

Forecast top 500 SKUs per store for 12 weeks. Factor in 2 years of sales, planned promotions, holidays, and external signals (weather, events) via web search. Plain English, not ML notebooks.

03

Dynamic Pricing & Promotions

Flag products priced >10% above market via Browser Use competitor checks. Cross-reference with inventory: which overpriced products have excess stock? Recommend adjustments with margin impact estimates.

04

Cross-Channel Inventory Optimization

Identify products where online sell-through is 2× in-store rate but store inventory is higher. Calculate lost online revenue from stockouts and rebalancing cost. Join e-commerce velocity + in-store levels + warehouse stock.

05

Category & Assortment Performance

Omnichannel sell-through rate, gross margin, repeat purchase rate, and cannibalization index by category. Are new products stealing from existing ones or generating incremental revenue? Transaction-level cross-channel analysis.

06

Retail Media Network ROI

True ROAS: ad spend vs. incremental sales (campaign period sales minus baseline). Break down by brand, category, and ad format. Join ad impressions, POS transactions, and baseline data for real incrementality measurement.

Honest Framing: When InfiniSynapse is not the right choice

You likely don't need InfiniSynapse if: (1) You are single-channel (e-commerce only or physical only) — channel-native tools may suffice. (2) You already have a retail data platform (Databricks, Snowflake + retail model) with POS, e-commerce, loyalty, and inventory unified — InfiniSynapse's strongest ROI is compressing time-to-answer. (3) Your questions are operational ("yesterday's in-store sales?") not analytical ("which omnichannel customers are most valuable and how do we find more?"). InfiniSynapse creates the most value when questions require joining 3+ channel systems.

FAQ

Can InfiniSynapse unify POS and e-commerce data?
Yes — connects directly to POS databases, e-commerce platforms (Shopify, Magento), loyalty databases, and inventory systems. Ask "which customers buy in-store AND online, and what's their combined LTV?" without building a warehouse first.
How does demand forecasting work for retail?
InfiniSynapse combines historical sales, promotional calendars, seasonal patterns, and external factors via web search (weather, competitor activity, trends) for store-SKU-level forecasts. Knowledge Base stores your methodology for consistency across categories.
How does this compare to Databricks for retail?
Databricks excels at large-scale data processing and ML on a lakehouse — but requires significant engineering investment. InfiniSynapse connects directly to sources without pre-modeling, uses natural language, and is designed for retail analysts and category managers, not just data engineers.

Customer 360 (POS + ecom + loyalty), store-SKU demand forecasting, dynamic pricing (elasticity + competitor + inventory), cross-channel inventory optimization, category/assortment performance, and retail media network ROI with true incrementality measurement.
Is our retail and customer data secure?
Yes. Air-Gapped private deployment keeps all POS transactions, customer PII, loyalty data, inventory levels, and pricing strategies on your own servers. Essential for PCI-compliant transaction data, customer PII, and competitive pricing under GDPR.
