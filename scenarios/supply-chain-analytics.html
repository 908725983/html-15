Supply Chain Disruptions

Three data disconnects that delay every shipment

Supply chain teams don't lack data — they lack connected data. Each system knows its piece. No system sees the full picture.

Critical · Systems Gap

OTIF needs 3 systems that don't talk

On Time In Full requires purchase orders (ERP), receiving data (WMS), and quality results (QMS/ERP). Most teams calculate OTIF monthly in Excel by exporting from all three and manually reconciling — too slow to catch issues before the next order cycle.

High · Blind Spot

Inventory optimization is blind to supplier reality

Safety stock formulas are straightforward — but optimal levels depend on supplier lead time variability (ERP), current stock across nodes (WMS), and demand signals (channels). Most teams optimize within one system, blind to the other two.

Medium · Reactive

Exceptions surface too late

When a shipment is late (TMS knows), inventory drops (WMS knows), and a PO is delayed (ERP knows) — but no system alerts the others. Teams discover exceptions by manually checking each system, or worse, when a customer order can't be fulfilled.

Core Workflows

Six analyses that run your supply chain control tower

The highest-frequency supply chain analytics workflows, spanning procurement, logistics, and planning.

01

OTIF Performance Tracking

Calculate OTIF by supplier, DC, and lane. Break down misses into late, short, and quality rejections. Join PO data (ERP), receipts (WMS), and quality data in one query.

02

Inventory Imbalance Detection

Identify SKUs with >30% imbalance across DCs relative to regional demand. Calculate rebalancing cost vs. carrying cost of inaction. WMS + demand planning joined.

03

Supplier Performance Scorecards

On-time %, fill rate, quality acceptance, lead time variability, and total cost of poor performance — ranked from best to worst. ERP + WMS + TMS joined.

04

Freight Cost Analysis

Cost per unit by lane and mode. Identify lanes with >15% QoQ increase. Break down rate changes vs. mix shift vs. utilization. TMS + ERP unit-cost allocation.

05

Demand Signal Intelligence

Compare actual demand vs. forecast by region. Where is demand exceeding forecast by >20%? Current inventory coverage at those nodes? Supplier flex capacity?

06

Perfect Order Rate Optimization

Orders on time, in full, damage-free, correct docs — by customer, DC, month. Which failure mode dominates? Cost of imperfection by mode. End-to-end across all systems.

Honest Framing: When InfiniSynapse is not the right choice

You likely don't need InfiniSynapse if: (1) You operate a single-DC, single-system supply chain — your ERP's native reporting covers most needs. (2) You already have a mature supply chain data warehouse with Fivetran-ingested ERP/WMS/TMS data and dbt-modeled metrics, with a proficient SQL team. (3) Your supplier base is under 20 with 2 or fewer DCs — the multi-system join problem is manageable in spreadsheets. InfiniSynapse creates the most value when questions require joining 3+ operational systems and when detection speed (daily vs. monthly) directly impacts service levels and logistics costs.

FAQ

Can InfiniSynapse connect to our ERP and WMS?
Yes — direct database connection (PostgreSQL, MySQL, SQL Server, Oracle), CSV export (SAP, Oracle EBS, Netsuite, Manhattan, Blue Yonder), or API. Auto-discovers schemas and joins inventory (WMS) with suppliers (ERP) and shipments (TMS) in a single query.
How is this different from ERP-native reporting?
ERP reporting shows what happened inside one system. It cannot answer "across our 3 DCs, which suppliers cause the most OTIF misses and what's the freight cost impact?" — that requires joining ERP, WMS, and TMS simultaneously, which no single system can do.
How does this compare to ThoughtSpot?
ThoughtSpot offers strong search-based analytics for supply chain — but requires data already modeled in a cloud warehouse. InfiniSynapse connects directly to operational systems without the warehouse prerequisite. Both offer natural language; the difference is the warehouse step in between.
What supply chain metrics can I analyze?
OTIF by supplier/DC/lane, inventory imbalance detection and optimization, supplier scorecards (delivery, quality, lead time), freight cost by lane/mode/carrier, Perfect Order Rate and failure mode trends, demand signal vs. supply reality checks — all by joining ERP, WMS, and TMS.
Is our supplier and cost data secure?
Yes. Air-Gapped private deployment keeps all ERP data, supplier contracts, inventory levels, and cost data on your own servers. Critical for teams managing competitive sourcing data and supplier pricing — unlike SaaS-only tools that require uploading to external clouds.
