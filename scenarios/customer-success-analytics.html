The Customer Journey

Where traditional CS analytics fails — a story in three acts

Every customer follows a journey. The data tells the story — if you can read it across the systems that hold each chapter.

Act I — The Silent Decline

Product usage drops 40% over 6 weeks. Nobody notices.

The customer's login frequency declined. Key features went unused. Power users stopped logging in. These signals existed — but in the product analytics database, which the CSM doesn't access. The CRM showed "all green." The support queue was quiet. By the time the CSM learned about the disengagement during a quarterly business review, the customer had already evaluated a competitor. The data was there. It just wasn't connected.

Act II — The Escalation Spiral

Support tickets spike. NPS drops. But no one connects the dots.

Three critical support tickets in 10 days. Two delayed responses. The NPS survey comes back at 4/10 — "support is unresponsive." But the CSM sees the account in CRM with no red flags. The support system and CRM don't share data. The NPS result goes to a different team. By the time someone manually connects "high support volume + low NPS = churn risk," the renewal conversation is next week and the customer has already decided not to renew.

Act III — The Expansion Opportunity Missed

Usage is 3x above plan. But the CSM is preparing a retention pitch.

Product usage is surging. The customer has added seats organically. They're using advanced features that indicate readiness for an upgrade. But the CSM is working from last quarter's stale account review showing "steady state." The expansion conversation never happens because the CS team doesn't have real-time visibility into the product signals that precede expansion. The customer eventually asks about upgrading — six months later than they would have, and with a competitor's quote in hand.

When InfiniSynapse is not the right choice for CS analytics

  • Your CS operation runs entirely within Gainsight or Totango. If all your customer data already flows into one CS platform and your team uses its playbooks and dashboards effectively, a purpose-built CS platform may be the better fit.
  • You have fewer than 20 accounts and high-touch relationships. In a high-touch, low-volume CS model where CSMs know every account intimately, automated health scoring may add less value than in a tech-touch or scaled CS model.
  • Your CS data already lives in a well-modeled data warehouse. If you've already invested in centralizing customer data and building CS analytics in a BI tool, InfiniSynapse may be additive rather than foundational.

InfiniSynapse creates the most value when CS teams need to join data across 3+ systems (CRM + product + support + NPS + billing), when churn signals exist across systems but no one is connecting them, and when CSMs spend more time compiling account reviews than engaging customers.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Common questions about AI customer success analytics.

How does InfiniSynapse calculate customer health scores?
InfiniSynapse computes health scores by joining data from CRM, product usage database, support ticket system, NPS surveys, and billing data. You define the health score formula once in the Knowledge Base, and the AI applies it consistently across all accounts.
Can InfiniSynapse predict which customers will churn?
Yes. InfiniSynapse analyzes cross-source signals — declining usage, increasing support tickets, NPS drops, delayed payments — to flag at-risk accounts. The AI identifies patterns that preceded past churn events and applies them to current accounts.
How does InfiniSynapse compare to Gainsight or Totango?
Gainsight and Totango are purpose-built CS platforms with pre-built playbooks and workflow automation. InfiniSynapse differentiates on data flexibility: it connects to any combination of databases and tools — not just what the CS platform ingests — for cross-source federation natively.
What is an Account 360 view?
An Account 360 is a single view of every customer dimension: product usage, support tickets, NPS trend, renewal date, contract value, billing history, and health score. InfiniSynapse builds it by joining data across all your customer systems and makes it queryable in plain English.
Is my customer data secure?
Yes. InfiniSynapse supports private deployment (Air-Gapped). All customer health data, usage logs, NPS responses, support tickets, and billing information stay on your own servers. Private deployment is critical for teams handling customer PII, contract details, or operating under SOC 2 and GDPR.
