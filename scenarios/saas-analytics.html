Six core SaaS analytics workflows

01
MRR/ARR Growth Decomposition
Decompose MRR into new, expansion, contraction, and churned by month. Identify which month had the highest gross churn and which customers drove it. The report that takes a finance analyst 2 days in Excel — now one query.
"Waterfall MRR growth by month for last 12 months"
02
NRR by Segment & Cohort
Calculate NRR by segment (SMB, Mid-Market, Enterprise) and plan tier for last 4 quarters. Separate expansion from churn. Identify which segment has NRR below 100% and the common traits of churned accounts.
"NRR by segment Q1-Q4 with churn characteristics"
03
LTV/CAC by Channel & Cohort
Compare LTV, CAC, and ratio by acquisition channel (paid search, organic, outbound, partner) for the last 4 quarters. Which channel has a payback period over 18 months? The analysis every board asks for — on demand.
"LTV/CAC by acquisition channel Q1 2025 through Q1 2026"
04
PLG Funnel Analytics
Full PLG funnel: signup → activation (milestone in 7d) → conversion (free → paid) → expansion (upgrade +2 seats). Breakdown by acquisition channel. Find the biggest drop-off and what behaviors predict passing each stage.
"PLG funnel conversion rates by channel for Q1"
05
Churn Prediction & Reason Analysis
Identify churned accounts in last 90 days. For each: MRR lost, support ticket volume in 60d before churn, usage trend in 30d before churn, and primary cancellation reason. Transforms churn from a number into something actionable.
"Churn post-mortem: who churned, why, and what were the leading indicators?"
06
Feature-Retention Correlation
Correlate feature usage (which features, frequency, user count) with 12-month retention. Top 5 features with strongest positive correlation vs. features used by churned accounts but rarely by retained — revealing UX or value communication gaps.
"Feature adoption vs. retention: which features actually drive retention?"

When InfiniSynapse is not the right choice for SaaS analytics

Honest framing — you likely don't need InfiniSynapse if:

  • You only need billing metrics (MRR, churn, LTV from Stripe alone). ChartMogul, Baremetrics, and ProfitWell are purpose-built for subscription revenue analytics and do it well.
  • You're pre-product-market-fit with under 50 customers. A spreadsheet works fine. InfiniSynapse's value grows with customer count (200+) and data source count (3+ systems).
  • You already have a full modern data stack with a dedicated analytics team. Fivetran → Snowflake → dbt → BI tool. InfiniSynapse's strongest value is compressing the path from "data in 3+ silos" to "trusted SaaS metrics" for teams without a data engineering function.

InfiniSynapse creates the most value when your SaaS analytics questions require joining 3+ data sources, when metric definitions need governance, and when the speed of answers (5 min vs. 5 days) directly impacts business decisions.

FAQ

Can InfiniSynapse connect to Stripe and other billing systems?
Yes. InfiniSynapse connects to your billing system's database (Stripe, Chargebee, Recurly all back onto PostgreSQL or MySQL), your product usage database, your CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot), and your support ticketing system (Zendesk, Intercom). It auto-discovers schemas and joins subscription revenue with product usage, sales pipeline, and support tickets — all in one query, without ETL or data warehouse modeling.
What SaaS metrics can InfiniSynapse calculate?
Core SaaS metrics: MRR/ARR (with new, expansion, contraction, churned breakdown), net revenue retention (NRR), gross and net churn rate, LTV/CAC ratio by cohort and channel, CAC payback period, expansion revenue rate, feature adoption and DAU/WAU/MAU, PLG funnel conversion rates, and support ticket trends correlated with churn risk. All metrics are defined once in your Knowledge Base and calculated consistently across every analysis.
How is this different from SaaS BI tools like ChartMogul or Baremetrics?
ChartMogul and Baremetrics specialize in subscription revenue analytics — MRR, churn, LTV — from your billing data. They are excellent at what they do but are limited to billing data. They cannot answer: "Which product features do our highest-NRR customers use that our churned customers didn't?" These questions require joining billing with product usage with support data — InfiniSynapse's multi-source federation enables this. ChartMogul/Baremetrics = billing-only metrics. InfiniSynapse = cross-source SaaS intelligence.
Can InfiniSynapse handle PLG (product-led growth) metrics?
Yes. PLG metrics require joining product usage (who signed up, activated, hit the paywall) with billing (who converted, at what plan) with CRM (sales-assisted expansions). No single SaaS tool connects all three. InfiniSynapse analyzes the full PLG funnel: signup → activation → conversion → expansion — and correlates each stage transition with specific product behaviors, acquisition channels, and account characteristics.
Is our customer and financial data secure?
Yes. InfiniSynapse supports private deployment (Air-Gapped). All billing data, subscription records, customer PII, product usage logs, and support ticket data stay on your own servers, processed locally. Essential for SaaS companies subject to SOC 2, GDPR, or enterprise DPAs that prohibit third-party data access — which includes most B2B SaaS companies above $10M ARR.
