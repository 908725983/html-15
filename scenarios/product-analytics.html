When InfiniSynapse is not the right choice

You only need funnel analytics. If your questions are "how many users completed onboarding this week?", dedicated tools like Amplitude or PostHog are purpose-built for this. InfiniSynapse differentiates on cross-source analysis.
You have a mature warehouse + dbt. If your data team already modeled everything into a warehouse with a semantic layer, a warehouse-native BI tool may be smoother. InfiniSynapse's advantage is compressing time-to-insight.
Your data is single-source. If all product questions are answered from one database, multi-source federation is overkill. InfiniSynapse creates the most value when questions span 3+ systems that don't talk to each other.

FAQ

Can InfiniSynapse connect to Amplitude or Mixpanel?
Yes, via SQL access to your product analytics warehouse or CSV export. InfiniSynapse joins Amplitude/Mixpanel event data with your production DB, CRM, support tickets, and surveys in a single query — something dedicated analytics tools cannot do.
How is this different from warehouse-based analytics?
Warehouse-based product analytics assumes data is already centralized. That requires 2-6 months of data engineering. InfiniSynapse connects directly to sources without pre-modeling. Time-to-first-insight: days, not months.
What questions can I answer that I can't today?
Cross-source questions: which features predict retention (usage + CRM), whether support-heavy users adopt differently (usage + tickets), whether NPS detractors share usage patterns (usage + NPS). These require 3-4 systems joined — exactly what multi-source federation automates.
How does this compare to Hex?
Hex is a notebook platform requiring SQL/Python — powerful for data teams but not self-serve for PMs. Hex assumes warehouse-first. InfiniSynapse combines multi-source connection with natural language querying, usable directly by PMs and product ops.
Is my product data secure?
Yes. InfiniSynapse supports private deployment (Air-Gapped). All product usage data and user behavior logs stay on your servers, processed locally. Critical for teams handling PII or operating under GDPR/SOC 2.
