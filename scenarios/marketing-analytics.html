Three Hard Problems with Fragmented Marketing Data

Marketing teams don't lack data — they lack unified data. These problems affect teams spending anywhere from $50K to $50M per month.

1

Each platform reports its own truth

Google uses 30-day click-through attribution. Meta uses 7-day click + 1-day view. Your email platform counts anyone who clicked a link. Your CRM records revenue 45 days after the first ad click. Four platforms, four versions of "this channel drove X conversions" — and none of them reconcile against actual revenue.

2

Attribution breaks when ad data and revenue data are in separate systems

Last-click attribution credits 100% to the final touchpoint. But a customer might discover you via LinkedIn, nurture via email for 3 weeks, search your brand, and finally convert. Without joining ad impressions + email engagement + CRM revenue, you can't build a real multi-touch model.

3

Budget optimization is reactive because reporting is slow

Most teams review weekly or monthly because compiling the data takes that long. By the time you see a Meta campaign's CPA spiked 40%, another 7 days of budget have been wasted. Real-time optimization requires the data to already be unified — and that's the hard part.

Six Core Marketing Analytics Workflows

Workflow 1

Cross-Channel ROAS and Campaign Performance — campaign performance analysis

"Compare ROAS, CPA, and conversion volume across Google Ads, Meta Ads, and email for the last 30 days. Flag any campaign with CPA more than 50% above the channel average." InfiniSynapse pulls spend, CRM revenue, and email engagement in one pass.

Workflow 2

Multi-Touch Attribution — multi-touch attribution

Define your model once in the Knowledge Base, then ask: "For Q1 enterprise deals, show channel contribution using a 40/20/20/20 time-decay model over a 90-day lookback." Joins ad impressions, email timestamps, and CRM opportunity dates across tens of thousands of touchpoints.

Workflow 3

Budget Waste Identification — marketing budget optimization

"Identify campaigns spending over $5K/month with CPA more than 2x channel average and conversion rate declining for 3 consecutive weeks. Recommend reallocation amounts." Cross-channel waste detection that no single-platform dashboard can do.

Workflow 4

CAC & LTV by Channel & Cohort — customer acquisition cost analysis

"Compare CAC, 30-day LTV, and 90-day LTV by channel and monthly cohort for the last 6 months. Which channel's LTV/CAC ratio is improving? Which is deteriorating?" Cross-source join that requires either a mature data warehouse — or InfiniSynapse.

Workflow 5

Automated Performance Reports — automated marketing reports

Set once: "Send a daily marketing summary to #growth Slack: spend, conversions, CPA, ROAS by channel vs. yesterday and vs. same day last week. Flag deviations over 30%." Runs cross-source analysis on schedule and pushes results to Slack or email.

Workflow 6

Engagement & Conversion Lift — engagement rate analysis

"For users who engaged with 3+ marketing emails before converting vs. first-touch converters, compare 90-day LTV, product adoption rate, and churn rate." Connects email data + CRM + product usage — telling you which campaigns drive valuable conversions, not just any conversions.

When InfiniSynapse Is Not the Right Choice

  • You spend primarily on 1-2 channels with simple attribution. If you're a startup spending $20K/month on Meta and Google, and last-click is good enough, platform dashboards + a spreadsheet work fine.
  • You already have a mature marketing data stack. Fivetran → Snowflake → dbt → BI tool with a built attribution model. InfiniSynapse's strongest value is compressing the path from scattered data to unified truth.
  • Your questions are single-channel and tactical. "How did our Google brand campaign do yesterday?" is handled perfectly by the Google Ads dashboard.

InfiniSynapse creates the most value when your marketing questions require joining 3+ data sources that don't naturally talk to each other — and when optimization speed directly impacts ROI.
